Dansk IT / Virk

Hackathon / Concept
my role: research, UX, fast prototyping, concept development

In 2017 I participated in the Hackathon organised by Dansk It. I was part of a team of four and had as a case the functionality of Virk.dk.
‍
‍
Task
The given task was to improve the user experience for the platform. The outlined focus was on navigation and quick information access.


Problem
The user segment we had in focus were small to medium businesses that stuggled with both having an overview over the entire process of administrating their business and finding the relevant information at the right moment of the journey.
‍

Aim
The aim we outlined was that we were to support business in their pursue of accessing necessary information in good time for them to adhere to mandatory deadlines. This was seen as the first step in improving the overall experience.


Insights
The insights were extracted by having conversations with the representatives from the company and were outlined as:

  • information about the necessary steps for the process of maintaining a business up and running were scattered; essential actions were easy to overlook
  • the information was experienced by the users as being an overload
  • there was an increased amount of tasks in certain time periods which made challenging for the users to plan actions ahead while missing the overview
  • users struggled in their attempt to deliver all the neccessary information and documentation as part of the mandatory process

Process
Based on the insights from the client and a speedy desktop research we began to outline the journey that a user would have to go through. We complemented that with the different other stakeholders what the user would need to interact with during this process. Then we approaced the task of filtering information in order to be able to extract the necessary one as fast as possible. We considered different ways of filtering and re-organising the information. As we did not have direct access to users, we used our own network to find out additional informaiton about the experience of interacting with the platform.

Challenge
The biggest challenge was information arhitecture. While the information was spread around the platform and in different digital formatios, a search box or filtering seemed to not provide enough access.

Solution
We outlined a way to deliver better overview and access to information through the use of staged information visualisation and automatisation in the shape of a a bot. The information visualisation was presented as a interactive page in which the user could to reveal the steps and deadlines in the temporal order, while the bot helped with improving the navigation by using a natural language.

The solution was rewarded with 2nd prize at the Offentlig Digitalisering Denmark conference in Aarhus in the same year.
‍

Future
The solution could have been improved by providing a customised view of the journey as part of the user profile, delivering incresingly more precise information in order to complement general overview.
